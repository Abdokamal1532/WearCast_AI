ROOT CAUSE A — BUG-01 & BUG-07: Armpit Hole / Silhouette Clipping
(utils_wearcast.py → get_mask_location)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Code path:
  Line ~55:   inpaint_mask  = upper_clothes (4) | dress (7)
  Line ~65:   arms          = left_arm (14) | right_arm (15) → OR'd into mask
  Line ~90:   mask_expanded = cv2.dilate(inpaint_mask, kernel, iterations=1)
  Line ~92:   mask_expanded = cv2.bitwise_and(mask_expanded, silhouette)
              ^ This "SILHOUETTE LOCK" clips the mask to only parsed foreground.
  Line ~96:   mask_expanded[skin_protect == 255] = 0
              ^ Arm skin (14/15) is subtracted BACK from the mask after having
                been added as coverage. For "upperbody" category the skin_protect
                does NOT include arm labels (correct), but the sequence of
                add-arms → dilate → AND-silhouette → subtract-skin can leave
                the armpit transition zone (the ~20-30px junction between label 4
                and label 14/15) with incomplete coverage, especially when:
                  - Subject is wearing a short-sleeve shirt (arm label starts
                    immediately below sleeve hem, reducing overlap).
                  - Subject has slim build (silhouette boundary is narrow).

Specific failure mechanism for BUG-01:
  After dilation, the junction zone between UpperClothes (4) and the Arm labels
  (14/15) is ≤ dilation_kernel_size/2 pixels wide. When the silhouette lock
  clips this zone AND the skin_protect zeros out arm pixels, a thin wedge
  remains uncovered at the armpit. The UNet inpaints this wedge as background.

Specific failure mechanism for BUG-07 (Abdo):
  Abdo's black henley has garment_pixel_pct in the 15-22% range, triggering the
  25-pixel dilation kernel (the aggressive branch). However, the silhouette lock
  (bitwise_and with silhouette) immediately clips the dilated zone on the lateral
  sides of the torso because the parsed silhouette ends at the shirt edge.
  The dilation expansion is effectively cancelled by the silhouette clip for slim
  subjects, leaving the lateral boundary under-masked.

ROOT CAUSE C — BUG-03: Black Garment Colour Drift
(inference_wearcast_hd.py → VAE encode/decode cycle, LAB compositing)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Multiple contributing factors:

C1. VAE Reconstruction Bias for Near-Black Colours
    The SD1.5 VAE (fp16 mode) has documented difficulty reconstructing very low
    luminance (L < 10 in LAB) values accurately. The encoder maps near-black
    regions to a compressed latent space region, and the decoder introduces
    a slight hue shift toward cool/green tones upon reconstruction. This is an
    inherent limitation of the SD1.5 VAE when operating in float16.

C2. CLIP Vision Embedding Ambiguity for Monochrome Garments
    The CLIP ViT-L/14 encoder processes the masked garment image. When the
    garment is near-uniform black (with only subtle white lettering), the global
    CLIP embedding is dominated by luminosity features rather than hue/chroma
    features. The UNet-Garm conditioning signal is therefore weak for colour,
    and the UNet-Vton is free to drift toward whatever hue it finds most
    probable given the person's skin tone and background — in this case, olive.

C3. Composite Luminance Mismatch
    In prepare_vton_latents() (pipeline_wearcast.py, line ~958-960):
      mask_vae_bin = (mask_vae > 0.5).to(dtype=image.dtype)
      image_masked = image * one_minus_mask
    For a dark garment, the masked region (set to 0) is already close to the
    original pixel value. The UNet receives a near-zero latent as the "empty"
    inpainting signal. This creates an unusual inpainting conditioning vs. what
    the model was trained on (which typically saw bright 127-grey fills in the
    masked region for standard inpainting training). The model compensates by
    generating a brighter-than-black result.

C4. Interaction with Over-Sized Mask (BUG-02 + BUG-03)
    Because the mask for Subject 2 is too large (BUG-02), the UNet must
    generate a much larger background/garment area, pulling in context from the
    green grass background visible at the lateral mask boundaries. This green
    environmental colour leaks into the generated garment hue.

ROOT CAUSE D — BUG-04: Old Garment Colour Bleed at Lateral Edges
(utils_wearcast.py → dilation + silhouette lock interaction)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Same mechanism as Root Cause A but in the compositing phase.
After final compositing (inference_wearcast_hd.py, lines 845-849):

The alpha_3d is derived from the hard_mask_resized with a 7px GaussianBlur

(inference_wearcast_hd.py → UNet resolution + TPS warping disabled)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
detect_garment_complexity() is permanently hardcoded to return False

FIX-01 | Armpit Junction Bridging (Addresses BUG-01, BUG-04, BUG-07)
File: utils_wearcast.py → get_mask_location()
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Problem: The arm-OR step and the dilation kernel leave the armpit zone partially
uncovered after the silhouette lock clips the dilation expansion.

Solution A — Morphological Closing Before Silhouette Lock:
  After combining inpaint_mask | arms, apply a morphological CLOSE (not just
  dilate) operation before the silhouette AND:

    combined = cv2.bitwise_or(inpaint_mask, arms)
    # Close fills concavities (like the armpit indent) better than pure dilation
    kernel_close = np.ones((dilation_kernel_size, dilation_kernel_size), np.uint8)
    mask_closed = cv2.morphologyEx(combined, cv2.MORPH_CLOSE, kernel_close)
    # Apply silhouette lock AFTER close
    mask_expanded = cv2.bitwise_and(mask_closed, silhouette)

Solution B — Convex-Hull Fill Over Shoulder Region:
  Compute the bounding box of (UpperClothes | Arms) and flood-fill the convex
  hull of the combined region. This guarantees no concavity is left at the
  armpit without relying solely on kernel size:

    from scipy.spatial import ConvexHull
    coords = np.column_stack(np.where((parse_array == 4) | 
                                       (parse_array == 14) | 
                                       (parse_array == 15)))
    if len(coords) >= 3:
        hull = ConvexHull(coords)
        hull_mask = np.zeros_like(inpaint_mask)
        cv2.fillPoly(hull_mask, [coords[hull.vertices][:, ::-1]], 255)
        inpaint_mask = cv2.bitwise_or(inpaint_mask, hull_mask)

Solution C (Quickest) — Bilateral Dilation: Apply a second-pass dilation
  specifically targeting the arm–torso junction after the skin_protect subtraction
  but before the silhouette lock. Use a VERY SMALL kernel (5x5) to re-fill the
  junction without expanding the mask globally:

    junction_region = cv2.dilate(arms, np.ones((5,5), np.uint8), iterations=2)
    junction_region = cv2.bitwise_and(junction_region, silhouette)
    mask_expanded = cv2.bitwise_or(mask_expanded, junction_region)

Recommendation: Implement Solution C first (1-hour change) to unblock the
pipeline, then graduate to Solution B for production quality.

FIX-02 | Dynamic Mask Cap for Oversized Garments (Addresses BUG-02)
File: utils_wearcast.py → get_mask_location()
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Problem: For boxy/oversized garments, the combined (UpperClothes | Arms) mask
is too large, and dilation makes it worse.

Solution:
  1. Cap the mask's lower boundary using the hip keypoints from OpenPose:
       hip_y = max(pose_data[8][1], pose_data[11][1])  # RHip, LHip keypoints
       # Scale to output resolution
       hip_y_scaled = int(hip_y * scale_factor)
       # Zero out anything below the hip line
       mask_expanded[hip_y_scaled:, :] = 0

  2. Add an explicit bottom-protection step that preserves the pants region
     regardless of dilation:

       pants_protect_dilated = cv2.dilate(pants_protect, np.ones((10,10), np.uint8))

       shoulder_width = abs(pose_data[2][0] - pose_data[5][0]) * scale_factor

               mask_expanded[:, :mask_cols[0] + trim] = 0

FIX-05 | Re-enable Spatial Logo Warping for Graphic Garments (Addresses BUG-05)
File: inference_wearcast_hd.py → detect_garment_complexity()
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
detect_garment_complexity() always returns False (permanently disabled).
The comment at line 884 explains this was done to avoid white-background halos.
The underlying apply_logo_warping() function itself is sound — the halo problem
was caused by pasting the raw garment image. The fix is to use only the
extracted logo pixels (not the full garment image) in the warp:

  In apply_logo_warping(), at the WARP & BLEND section (line ~279):
    # Replace:
    warped_garm = cv2.warpPerspective(garm_np, M, (w_gen, h_gen), ...)
    # With:
    # Warp only the logo pixels; use the GENERATED IMAGE as base (no white halo):
    logo_only = gen_np.copy()
    logo_pixels = (raw_logo_mask > 127)
    logo_only[~logo_pixels] = 0
    warped_logo = cv2.warpPerspective(logo_only, M, (w_gen, h_gen), ...)
    # Then alpha-composite warped_logo onto gen_np (not garm_np):
    result = gen_np * (1.0 - alpha_3d) + warped_logo * alpha_3d

  Re-enable the complexity detector with a meaningful threshold:
    def detect_garment_complexity(self, image_garm):
        garm_np = np.array(image_garm.convert('RGB')).astype(np.float32)
        bg_mask = np.all(garm_np >= 240, axis=-1)
        fg = garm_np[~bg_mask]
        if len(fg) < 100:
            return False
        base_color = np.median(fg, axis=0)
        color_diff = np.linalg.norm(garm_np - base_color, axis=-1)
        color_diff[bg_mask] = 0
        logo_coverage = np.sum(color_diff > 20) / max(1, (~bg_mask).sum())
        is_complex = logo_coverage > 0.08
        print(f"   [COMPLEXITY] logo_coverage={logo_coverage:.2%} → complex={is_complex}")
        return is_complex

OBS-1 | Scheduler Choice
  DDPMScheduler is loaded initially but immediately replaced with UniPCMultistep
  (inference_wearcast_hd.py, line 135). The DDPMScheduler weights are loaded
  for nothing. Minor VRAM and startup-time waste. Consider loading
  UniPCMultistepScheduler directly.

OBS-2 | torch.compile Scope
  Only unet_vton is compiled (line 150). unet_garm is not. Since unet_garm runs
  once per inference (garment encoding), compiling it as well would further
  reduce per-request latency.

OBS-3 | rembg Session Reuse
  rembg session is correctly initialized once in __init__ and reused. Good.
  However, the session type is hardcoded as "u2net". For garments with complex
  backgrounds, "isnet-general-use" yields sharper boundaries. Consider making
  this configurable.

OBS-4 | Float16/Float32 Mixed Precision Boundary
  CLIP Vision model is loaded in float32 (line 110: intentionally, comment says
  "matches original OOTD"). VAE is in float16. The image_encoder outputs
  float32 tensors. These are passed into the pipeline which operates in float16.
  The implicit float32→float16 cast happens without a warning. This is currently
  fine but should be made explicit with .to(torch.float16) at the boundary to
  prevent accidental dtype promotion in future code changes.

OBS-5 | Parsing Model Accuracy for Atypical Garments
  The human parsing model (ATR-LIP or similar) is known to misclassify oversized
  garments, garments with large graphics, and garments under harsh lighting.
  Consider adding a parsing confidence check: if the UpperClothes (label 4)
  pixel count is anomalously low (< 5% of image), flag the result and fall back
  to a bounding-box heuristic for the mask region rather than producing an
  incorrect segmentation-based mask.
